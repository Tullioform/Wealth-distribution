# Wealth distribution via kinetic exchange models

The kinetic theory of gases is a stochastic model concerning the distribution of energy within a gas. However, we will see that the principles of this formalism can also be applied to the study of problems in economics and social science. The idea is that a large number of molecules and a large social group share common characteristics; in particular, both are predictable precisely because of their intrinsic randomness.

## Features

- Homogeneous models with energy (wealth) conservation laws
- Boltzmann/Gibbs and Gamma distribution analysis
- Pareto model for wealth distribution
  - Dragulescu/Yakovenko model of wealth exchange
  - Chakraborti/Chakrabarti model
- "Social" particles exchange
  - Lallouache model
 
- Heterogeneous models

- Newton-Raphson data analysis

- Various social science problems: debt, fixed savings, taxes, rock-paper-scissors models
